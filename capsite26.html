Project Abstract

The Scope of EruvGuard

EruvGuard is a student-developed engineering project designed to address a critical and underserved need within Modern Orthodox Jewish communities: the reliable, real-time monitoring of an eruv. An eruv is a symbolic boundary — typically a continuous wire or string — that permits certain activities on Shabbat and Jewish holidays. Because even a single break in this boundary renders the entire eruv invalid, maintaining its integrity is an ongoing religious and logistical responsibility. Traditional inspection methods require dedicated volunteers to physically walk or drive the eruv perimeter each week, an effort that consumes significant time and is prone to human error.

EruvGuard addresses this challenge through an integrated hardware-software system that uses an accelerometer to detect positional changes in the eruv line, a solar-powered ESP32 microcontroller to process and transmit data, and a web-based alert interface to instantly notify community leaders when a disruption is detected. The system stores excess solar energy in rechargeable lithium batteries, ensuring continuous operation regardless of weather conditions.

Existing automated eruv systems — such as those deployed in Manhattan — can cost upwards of $200,000 annually and are custom-built for specific eruvin, making them inaccessible to the vast majority of communities. EruvGuard's goal is to deliver a low-cost, universally adaptable, and low-maintenance alternative that makes automated eruv supervision achievable for communities of any size, anywhere.

Technology

How EruvGuard Works

Four integrated components form a self-sustaining, always-on monitoring system that attaches directly to an existing eruv line.

01
Fall Detection

Accelerometer Monitoring

An accelerometer continuously tracks the position of the eruv line. When a break occurs, the device registers a significant positional change and immediately triggers an alert — distinguishing real breaks from normal environmental movement.

02
Alert System

ESP32 Web Interface

An ESP32 microcontroller processes signals from the accelerometer and transmits real-time alerts to an HTML-based web dashboard. The eruv manager receives an immediate, location-specific notification the moment a disruption is detected.

03
Renewable Energy

Solar Panel Power

A compact solar panel provides continuous, renewable power to the ESP32 and all connected components during daylight hours — eliminating manual battery replacements and keeping the system fully self-sufficient in the field.

04
Reserve Power

Lithium Battery Backup

Rechargeable lithium batteries store excess solar energy to ensure EruvGuard operates continuously through the night and on overcast days — so no break goes undetected regardless of conditions.

Research & Context

What Already Exists — and Why It's Not Enough

EruvGuard did not emerge in a vacuum. Before developing our system, we researched existing eruv monitoring technology and studied established eruv organizations to understand the current landscape of available solutions.

Some large-scale eruvin — most notably the Manhattan Eruv — have implemented forms of automated monitoring to assist with maintenance. These systems confirm that automated eruv supervision is both technically possible and halachically validated. The Manhattan Eruv's infrastructure serves as an important proof-of-concept, and we acknowledge their pioneering work.

However, existing automated systems carry significant limitations. Their high cost — estimated at approximately $200,000 per year — places them out of reach for the vast majority of communities. They are also custom-built for a single, specific eruv and cannot be redeployed without substantial reinvestment.

EruvGuard is designed to close this gap: an affordable, modular, and community-agnostic solution that any eruv can adopt without prohibitive financial investment.

Who It's For

Target Audience & Limitations

EruvGuard is built for any Jewish community with an eruv — large or small, urban or suburban. The size of your eruv does not matter.

  • 🕍
    Rabbis & Religious Authorities Receive instant, halachically relevant alerts and manage eruv validity with confidence — no more end-of-week uncertainty.
  • 👥
    Eruv Inspectors & Volunteers Replace exhausting weekly perimeter walks with targeted, alert-driven maintenance — saving hundreds of hours each year.
  • 🏘️
    The Wider Jewish Community Any community member who relies on a valid eruv for Shabbat benefits from a more dependable, consistently maintained boundary.

Known Limitations

Weight: If the device is too heavy, it could strain or snap thin eruv strings. The team is minimizing weight through design iteration and lighter materials.

Size: A device that is too large or bulky could interfere with the eruv line. Future versions aim to significantly reduce form factor.

Weather resistance: Current housing uses PLA filament, which is less weather-resistant. A planned upgrade to ALA filament will improve outdoor durability.

Pressure sensing: Current version detects positional changes. A planned load cell will enable direct tension sensing for even greater accuracy.
